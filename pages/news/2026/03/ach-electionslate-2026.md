All current members of ACH are eligible to vote. Those wanting to vote in the election must join or renew their membership (if expired) by March 20, 2026. Voting is anticipated to open April 1 via a Qualtrics survey that will be emailed directly to all members.

To check your membership status:
1. Visit [https://members.ach.org/](https://members.ach.org/)
1. On the Membership dropdown menu, select “Login”
1. Once logged in, hover over the same “Membership” menu and select “Member Dashboard”
1. Scroll to the bottom to confirm your membership expiration date
1. If you need to renew, you can do so by clicking on the [“renew your membership”](https://members.ach.org/join/) link at the top of the page. If you experience problems renewing, you may need to log out.

# Nominees for ACH Co-Vice Presidents/Co-Presidents Elect

## 1. Eduard Arriaga
****Bio:**** Eduard Arriaga is Associate Professor and Chair of the Department of Language, Literature and Culture at Clark University. He has published six books and numerous articles on literature, literary theory and digital humanities, Afro-Latinx and Black digital humanities in outlets such as DHQ, Debates in the Digital Humanities, Hispania, etc. His most recent co-edited volume, Afro-Latinx Digital Connections (Florida Press, 2021) is a collection of essays and interviews that explore ways in which Black Latinx communities engage our digital world. He is currently working on a book project under contract with Vanderbilt University Press, tentatively titled Afro-Brazilian Community Data Networks.

****Candidate Statement:**** I am excited to stand for election for the role of Vice-President of the ACH, so I can continue to help making the association and the digital humanities spaces where care and community building go hand in hand with the development of scholarly and professional goals. I have been honored to serve as an ACH council representative for the last four years in diverse capacities: member of the conference committee (2023, 2024, 2025), member of the professional development committee (2023 and 2024) liaison for the DH2025 (ADHO) Conference Program committee, and publications liaison (2026). All these experiences have allowed me not only to get to know better the association but also to explore the diversity of perspectives that are part of our growing digital humanities communities. As ACH Vice-President, I welcome the opportunity to continue placing social justice and human values at the center of ACH and the digital humanities as a complex field of research, practice, and scholarship. In this capacity, I also expect to bring my intercultural, multilingual, and international experience to continue making the ACH much more inclusive and expanded in its pursuing to fostering our shared humanity in an age of computational and algorithmic determinism.

## 2. Mandy Regan and Brandon Walsh
****Bio:**** Amanda (Mandy) Regan is an Assistant Professor of History and Interim Associate Director of the Digital History Ph.D. program at Clemson University—the nation's first. She has served as ACH Communications Officer since 2024 and as ACH Liaison to the American Historical Association. As a historian of the late 19th and 20th centuries, she specializes in women, gender, and sexuality as well as digital humanities methods and pedagogy. She is co-director of Mapping the Gay Guides, an award-winning digital history project that explores the historical geography of LGBTQ life in North America. Her scholarship appears in Digital Futures of Graduate Study in the Humanities, the Journal of Sport History, and the Journal of Digital History. Her book, Shaping Up: Physical Fitness Initiatives for Women, 1880-1965, is forthcoming with the University of Virginia Press.

Brandon Walsh is Head of Student Programs in the Scholars’ Lab, a part of the Digital Humanities Center in the University of Virginia Library. His primary research focuses on digital humanities pedagogy, looking at the ways it can reflect and enact infrastructural change in higher education. He was a regular instructor at HILT, and he serves on the editorial board of The Journal of Interactive Technology and Pedagogy. He has work published or forthcoming with Programming Historian, Insights, the Digital Library Pedagogy Cookbook, Pedagogy, Digital Pedagogy in the Humanities, and Digital Scholarship in the Humanities, among others. His current book project is entitled Embedded Pedagogies: Digital Humanities Teaching and the Infrastructure of Change (under review). He currently serves as the ACH liaison to the Modern Languages Association and previously as a member of ACH's Executive Council. 

****Candidate Statement:****
We believe professional organizations should be communities of care, and our current moment demands this work be active and intentional. ACH has modeled this approach through recent advocacy—mobilizing around NEH funding threats, organizing town halls, and creating resources for affected members. We're committed to sustaining and expanding this care-based approach to professional community by focusing on mutual aid and collective action that centers the needs of early-career scholars, students, and staff. 

We envision focusing our own efforts on three areas:

1. **Professional Development** - We resolve to work in partnership with other digital humanities training organizations as we build ACH into a space that empowers teachers and students working with emerging technologies. We will assess this year's pre-conference ACH workshop pilot and build it into a sustained space for professional and pedagogical development. 
1. **Digital Humanities in Public** - We need digital humanists in public now more than ever. During our term, we plan to build on previous rapid response efforts and training opportunities to prepare digital humanists to leverage their research and teaching in the public sphere. We also promise to shape a community that acts in solidarity with those who attempt to do so. 
1. **Impact of Artificial Intelligence** - We cannot ignore that artificial intelligence is reshaping higher education and digital humanities in ways that don't necessarily align with our values. AI is likely to affect all corners of digital humanities work from our research methods to our pedagogical practices and institutional relationships. We will remain committed to ensuring that ACH leads critical conversations about how humanistic values, ethics, and justice should guide our engagement with these technologies.

This statement is our own, but we envision this work as collective. We hope to use our term to elevate and enable the contributions and energies of our community. It takes all of us.
